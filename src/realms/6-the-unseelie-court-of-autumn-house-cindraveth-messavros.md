Cindraveth — Realm of the Unseelie Autumn Court
The realm of famine, want, and the beauty of dying things.

Cindraveth is autumn stripped of comfort.

No warm harvest fires.
No gentle falling leaves.
No abundance gathered before winter’s rest.

Only decline stretched endlessly toward ruin.

The realm lies beneath a sky of perpetual dusk where sunlight filters weakly through drifting ash clouds, staining the world bronze, copper, and dying red. Winds move constantly across the land, carrying the dry whisper of dead leaves and the faint scent of smoke from fields that never stop smouldering.

At first glance, Cindraveth appears beautiful.

Forests blaze with crimson and gold.
Lanterns glow softly through silver mist.
Great orchards spread across rolling hills.
Rivers gleam like molten amber beneath the fading light.

But everything is wrong.

Leaves curl black at their edges.
Fruit hangs swollen and rotting on branches.
Cornfields rustle though no wind touches them.
The land is starving slowly beneath its own splendour.

Nothing grows cleanly here.

Every harvest fails eventually.

Vesperith — Capital of the Unseelie Autumn Court. Pride of Messavros and 
Seat of House Cindraveth, rises from the centre of the realm likethe remains of a dying fire.

The city is built from blackened stone streaked with copper and deep ember-red crystal. Towers curve upward like charred branches clawing toward the darkening sky, their windows glowing faintly behind veils of drifting smoke.

Thousands of lanterns hang throughout the city.

Amber lanterns.
Crimson lanterns.
Lanterns shaped from hollowed gourds, bronze cages, or delicate boneglass.

They sway endlessly in the wind, casting trembling pools of light through the mist.

The streets of Vesperith are lined with vast granaries, silent marketplaces, and gardens filled with beautiful things slowly decaying:
roses collapsing into ash,
vineyards thick with sour grapes,
fountains filled not with water, but dark wine reflecting distorted stars.

Music drifts constantly through the city —
soft violins,
mourning pipes,
slow drums like weakening heartbeats.

The Court of House Messavros believes hungerreveals truth.

Not only hunger for food—
but for love,
power,
beauty,
safety,
hope.

In Cindraveth, deprivation is considered sacred.

They do not simply destroy abundance.

They withhold it.

The nobles of the Unseelie Autumn Court dress in magnificent layered fabrics the colour of dying leaves and burnt gold, adorned with amber jewels that trap tiny moving shadows within their depths. Their feasts overflow with exquisite food that often turns tasteless, spoiled, or ash-cold upon the tongues of unwanted guests.

Nothing in Vesperith is ever freely given.

Every gift creates debt.

Every comfort can be withdrawn.

🌾 The Blightfields
Beyond the capital stretch the Blightfields —
vast farmlands cursed by ancient scarcity magic.

Here wheat crumbles into grey dust at harvest.
Apples split open to reveal writhing black rot.
Rivers shrink overnight without warning.
Livestock wander thin and silent beneath scarecrows woven from old funeral cloth.

And yet, somehow, the fields remain hauntingly beautiful beneath the autumn light.

Golden enough to tempt hope.

Dead enough to destroy it.

The realm is known for:

• Scarcity magic tied to famine, rot, exhaustion, and decay.
• Curseweavers who unravel prosperity through whispered hexes and symbolic rituals.
• Lantern rites guiding spirits, bargains, and forgotten debts through the mist.
• Tactical warfare built on attrition, patience, and slow collapse rather than open conquest.
• Harvest feasts where glamour hides poison, hunger, or enchantment beneath beauty.

At the heart of Cindraveth lies its deepest truth:

The realm does not fear death.

It fears emptiness.

And so the Unseelie Autumn Court hoards,
withholds,
rations,
and watches—

while the world slowly starves around it.
