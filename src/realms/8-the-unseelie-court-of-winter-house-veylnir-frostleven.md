Stillness, coupled with Death's frost cover the void, eternal night that is Frostleven. Home to despair, forgetting and oblivion. Breath turns to ash. Frost burns black and its inhabitants erase with a merciless nihilistic voice.



Snow falls constantly.

Soft.

Soundless.

Endless.

And wherever it settles, colour fades.

Forests stand petrified beneath layers of black frost, their branches frozen into shapes like pleading hands. Rivers lie unmoving beneath glass-dark ice. Mountains loom across the horizon like sleeping titans buried beneath ash-white storms.

Even time feels wrong in Frostleven

Thoughts slow.
Voices weaken.
Memories blur at the edges.

The realm does not attack intruders immediately.

It simply waits for them to forget why they were resisting.

Veylstram  is Capital of the Unseelie Winter Court and Seat of House Veylnir.

Veylstram rises from the frozen plains like a city carved from mourning itself.

Immense towers of black ice pierce the sky, their surfaces smooth as mirrors yet swallowing reflection instead of returning it.Bridges arch between spires like skeletal ribs, draped in frost that glimmers faintly with dying silver light.

No warmth exists here.

No music.

No birdsong.

Only the hollow moan of wind threading through endless corridors and the distant tolling of bells beneath the ice.

The architecture of Veylstram is vast and severe:
cathedrals hollowed from obsidian glaciers,
courtyards buried beneath drifting snow,
great halls lit by pale blue flames that give no heat.

The city appears abandoned at first.

Then figures emerge from the snowfall:
silent nobles cloaked in layered silver-black garments,
faces pale as moonlit bone,
eyes empty with centuries of grief too old to name.

The rulers of House Veylnir believe oblivion ismercy.

Pain fades.
Love fades.
Fear fades.
Eventually, even identity itself disappears beneath the eternal winter.

And that, to them, is peace.

🌑 The Hollow Frost
Throughout Frostleven spreads the Hollow Frost —
the realm’s oldest and most feared magic.

This frost does not merely freeze flesh.

It consumes remembrance.

A single touch can blur names.
Long exposure erases faces, loyalties, promises.
Victims wander the snowfields unable to recall who they are or why theycontinue walking.

Breath crystallises black in the air beforeturning to drifting ash.

Some frozen statues scattered across the wastesare not statues at all—
but travellers who stopped remembering how to move.

🌨 The Vale of QuietGraves
Beyond Veylstram lies a vast plain of snow-covered ruins known as the Vale ofQuiet Graves.

Entire forgotten cities sleep beneath the icethere.

Bell towers protrude from glaciers.
Broken archways emerge during storms.
Ancient roads vanish beneath endless drifts.

Sometimes voices whisper beneath the snow.

Not screaming.

Calling gently.

Inviting wanderers to lie down and rest.

Many never rise again.

The realm is known for:

• Death-frost magic capable of freezing body, soul, and memory alike.
• Voidcraft, woven through shadow, silence, and emotional desolation.
• Black ice mirrors that erase reflections instead of revealing them.
• Funeral rites conducted beneath starless skies where names are surrendered to oblivion.
• Wraithbound guardians — silent knights frozen between life and death.

And at the heart of Frostleven stands the greatest terror of all:

not violence,
not monsters,
not even death—

but the slow and terrible possibility
that one day you might forget you were ever loved at all.
