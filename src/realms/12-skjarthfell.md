Far beyond the glittering towers of the Winter Court, where the frozen lands darken into the borders of Frostleven, the mountains of Skjarthfell rise from the snow like the broken teeth of some ancient god. Jagged, black, and half-buried beneath ice older than kingdoms, they stand untouched by warmth ,mercy, or time itself. No forests grow there. No birds cross its skies. Even the wind seems reluctant to linger.

The journey to Skjarthfell is long and merciless. Sleighs drawn by powerful winter hounds carry travellers across endless snowfields and frozen wastelands where the cold changes from the clean sharpness of Seelie Winter into something heavier — damp, suffocating, and wrong. The further north the travellers ride, the more the world seems stripped bare, as though life itself has been scraped from the land.

Yet the true danger of Skjarthfell is not the ice, nor the blizzards, nor even the creatures that stalk the mountain passes.

It is the mountain itself.

Ancient runes carved into the stone speak of laws older than the Courts of Fae. No magic may force the ascent. No power may claim what the mountain guards. Those who seek the summit must climb with nothing but flesh, breath, endurance, and truth. Skjarthfell does not care for titles, crowns, or strength borrowed through magic. It judges what lies beneath.

And deep within its silent passes dwells the Vraekhal — an ancient being of shadow and judgement, neither spirit nor stone, capable of stripping away deception until only truth remains. Beneath their gaze, secrets cannot remain hidden for long.

Somewhere beyond the frozen cliffs and silent paths lies the cure Gracie seeks — an elixir capable of saving Dara before the corruption of Thornhelm’s crown transforms him completely into something even more monstrous  than the King of Unseelie Summer.

But Skjarthfell has never surrendered its secrets freely.

And the mountain always takes something in return.
