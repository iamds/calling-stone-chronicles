Limskada is the Mortal Realm of Elyndra.  Ruled over by King Edmund and Queen Serena. Limskada is a land under attack. Its once beautiful landscape is marred and broken. Scarred by the darkness sent by Smerdante, hoping to force them to part with the Callisvaan secured by Temerin. 

It is under the protection of the Seelie Courts, Winter, Spring, Summer, Autumn, but even their magic seems powerless in stopping the darkness as it tries to overtake the land. 

It is from the castle of Limskada, that the Quest for the Callisvaan begins. Here Gracie meets the royal family, including Prince Dara and Prince Tavian

Limskada — Realm of Mortal Kings The land between worlds.

Limskada is only surviving mortal realm bordering the Passing Lands — the fragile veil between Earth and the FaeRealms. Unlike the Seelie and Unseelie Courts, Limskada possesses no true magic of its own. No glamour. No elemental dominion. No immortal bloodlines.

And because of that, it has always feared weakness.

For centuries, House Caelithorne ruled Limskada through discipline, bloodline purity, military order, and rigid tradition. Its people pride themselves on endurance and survival. Stone fortresses crown its mountains. Gold-threaded banners hang above ancient halls. Every law, every marriage, every inheritance is carefully controlled to preserve the strength of the realm.

But now Limskada is dying.

Smerdante’s darkness spreads through it likepoison beneath skin.

Not openly.
Not quickly.

Quietly.

The sky itself has begun to fracture.

At dusk, black cracks split the clouds like shattered glass, leaking strange violet light across the land. Crops fail without reason. Wells sour overnight. Villages empty slowly as despair settles into the hearts of the people. Men abandon homes. Mothers stop singing. Entire bloodlines vanish into silence.

The darkness whispers.

And eventually people begin listening.

Limska — Capital of Limskada Seat of House Caelithorne.

Limska was once a proud mountain capital of pale stone, blue banners, silver towers, and bustling market roads that connected the mortal realm to distant kingdoms beyond the deserts and forests.

Now it survives more than it lives.

The city rises behind immense curtain walls streaked black by damp and neglect. Towers lean slightly with age. Roofs sag inward. Smoke curls weakly from scattered chimneys while citizens move through narrow streets with their heads lowered, avoiding eye contact as though fear itself has become law.

Even sound feels muted in Limska.

Conversations stop when strangers pass.
Doors close quietly.
Prayers are whispered behind locked shutters.

The people make protective signs constantly:
against darkness,
against curses,
against despair.

Against hope lost too long ago.

Yet traces of former grandeur remain everywhere.

The royal castle still gleams faintly beneath its decay:
blue-painted halls veined with silver,
glass-bright polished floors,
ancient tapestries depicting forgotten beasts and heroic kings,
great chandeliers hanging like frozen constellations above empty corridors.

Gold and stone remain the symbols of Limskada:
mortal strength,
earthly power,
heritage carved to endure.

But even the castle feels haunted now.

The moat surrounding it lies stagnant and black, coated in unnatural slime where monstrous carrion-flies breed beneath the poisoned waters. Fungus creeps through the lower walls. Servants move silently through corridors heavy with exhaustion and fear.

It is a kingdom trying desperately to remember itself while darkness slowly teaches it to surrender.

 West of Limska lie the woodland and forests. An ancient woodland older than the kingdom itself bears no obvious name.

The forest forms a natural barrier between Limskada and the Passing Lands. Massive overlapping trees arch overhead so densely that daylight struggles to touch the earth. Pale mist coils endlessly between the roots, muffling footsteps and distorting direction.

Creatures dwell within it.

Things with too many eyes.
Too-silent wings.
Shifting antlers glimpsed between trees.

Some are merely territorial.

Others are hungry.

Travellers whisper that the forest changes according to fear. Paths vanish overnight. Voices imitate loved ones. Entire hunting parties disappear beneath the white mist without trace.

And somewhere deep within the woodland, ancient things still remember when the veil between worlds was thinner. The Woodland witch – the Caudisguiba dwells with the forest. 

Argenta — The SilverLands
Far south lies Argenta —
the vast silver desert of Limskada.

By day, the dunes shimmer beneath merciless sunlight like powdered metal. By night, moonlight transforms the sands into pale oceans rolling endlessly beneath the stars.

The Argenserpens dwell there. A great three headed silver-scaled serpent capable of vanishing beneath the dunes without sound. Its movement leaves ripples across the sand like water disturbed by unseen currents.

Wind  sings through the night.

Yet even here, Smerdante’s corruption spreads.

Oases dry overnight.
Mirages whisper false promises.
Moonlit sand sometimes blackens without warning beneath travellers’ feet.

Cael Ombryn — The Lake of Shadows
Near the borderlands lies Cael Ombryn, the Lake of Shadows.

Beautiful.
Silent.
Deadly.

Its waters mirror the night sky perfectly, no matter the weather above. Mist drifts constantly across its surface in slow twisting ribbons, and the lake seems almost alive in its stillness — listening, waiting. Here dwell the Deadly Sirens.

Many travellers report hearing whispers or song near the shore:
their own names,
lost memories,
voices of loved ones long dead.

Some walk willingly into the water.

Few return unchanged. If they return at all. 

The lake is feared even by the people of Limskada, for it lies dangerously close to the Passing Lands, where the veil between mortal and fae realms grows thin enough to bleed.

 The Passing Lands
At the furthest edges of Limskada stretch the Passing Lands —the fragile threshold between worlds.

Reality weakens there.

Mist hangs unnaturally thick.
Stars appear wrong.
Time moves strangely beneath certain moons.

Ancient standing stones mark forgotten crossings between Earth and the Fae Realms, though most are abandoned now or swallowed by the spreading darkness.

It is here that monsters cross most easily.

And it is here that Gracie’s arrival begins to change the fate of Limskada itself.

For despite all its decay,
its fear,
its pride,
and its stubborn cruelty—

Limskada is not yet lost.

Beneath the ash and ruin,
something still survives.

Waiting for light to return.
