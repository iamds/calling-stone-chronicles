King Corvalis and Queen Eiradene rule over this land. A place of hushed whispers, quiet contemplation and the preservation of knowledge. 

Ambersorin sits at the heart of Amberfall, a city carved from glowing stone that holds warmth of perpetual late - afternoon sunlight.

Its spires rise like gilded branches, each threaded with veins of living amber that catch the light and ripple gently as though stirred by some unseen breeze.

The streets wind through orchards, vineyards and tiered gardens overflowing with copper leaves and ripe fruit. Music drifts constantly through the air–soft pipes low drums, or the rustle of enchanted trees whispering their own harmonies. 

A vast amphitheatre, carved into a natural hollow and roofed with interlocking boughs of burnished cold, forms the city's ceremonial centre. Here, the Autumn Court deliberates, harvest rites are held, and the first winds of the season are welcomed in solemn dance. 

Ambersorin is known for: 

Warmth and hospitality, yet with a quiet melancholy as leaves fall

Ambercraft, where artisans trap memories, light or birdsong within crystallised sap

Fruit magic tied to ripening, decay and renewal. 

A deep connection to the balance of the Seelie realms– autumn as the bridge between life and decline. 

Though beautiful, the city carries an undercurrent of fragility:

The sense that everything is only moments from falling, drifting or turning to gold-dust under an autumn wind.
