Ystme is one of the oldest centres of magical learning in existence.Upon its shores stands the great sanctuary of the Grey Brethren, an order devoted to the pursuit of knowledge, balance and understanding. Within its halls, students study the mysteries of magic, history, astronomy and the ancient forces that shape the realms.

The island is renowned for its vast libraries, celestial observatories and rune-filled chambers, where knowledge gathered over countless generations is carefully preserved. Here, both Temerin and Smerdante once trained together before their paths diverged and their philosophies clashed.

Unlike many magical strongholds, Ystme values wisdom above power. Its teachings emphasise restraint, responsibility and the understanding that knowledge may illuminate or corrupt depending upon the intentions of the one who wields it.

For centuries, mages, scholars, and seekers of knowledge have travelled to its shores to study the ancient arts of spellcraft, herbal lore, rune magic, and the forgotten language of stone and starlight.

Among its most treasured possessions are *The Book of Marble* and *TheUntried Passages*, two ancient works whose pages contain wisdom, warningsand mysteries that have shaped the history of the realms. Few are permitted tostudy them, and fewer still truly understand the secrets they contain.

At the heart of the island stands the Great Observatory, where the stars are watched, charted, and woven into powerful enchantments. Rune-carved halls, and sacred chambers overlook the surrounding waters, their walls holding secrets gathered across countless generations.

It was here that Temerin first learned the magical arts, and here that he once studied alongside his closest friend, Smerdante, long before their paths diverged. Though the island remains a place of wisdom and discovery, it also stands as a quiet reminder that knowledge alone cannot determine a person's destiny.

Ystme is a place where magic is not merely taught—it is understood, questioned, and woven into the very fabric of the world itself. Beneath its ancient arches and star-lit ceilings, every lesson begins with a single truth: power is never as important as the choices made with it.

Symbolism

Ystme symbolises learning, wisdom and the choices that shape destiny. It represents the idea that knowledge itself is neither good nor evil, but becomes one or the other through the actions of those who possess it.
