Ebonreach is Smerdante's realm. The Unseelie Court of Spring. House Nyxareth. A realm of Secrets and Shadows.  Full of beauty and hidden deception. Its beauty is only an illusion for underneath its veneer, shadows peel from walls, whispers float like smoke and those who inhabit Ebonreach mask themselves in its deception. It shows itself to be elegant, attractive and alluring, the truth reveals monsters, false glamour and lies. 

Smerdante use magic to hide what he does not want seen.  He controls through manipulation, fear and suggestion. He does not play fair and shadows do his bidding. When Gracie finds herself there, she is in terrible danger.

Ebonreach–The realm of Illusion, temptation and beautiful ruin.  Ebonreach is not a land that reveals itself honestly.

it changes

Breathes.

Lies. 

At its borders, travellers may first glimpse paradise: flowering forests bathed in eternal twilight, silver rivers winding beneath skies painted in impossible shades of lilac and rose, distant towers gleaming like polished obsidian beneath drifting stars.

But nothing in Ebonreach remains true for long.

The skies change when unobserved. Paths curl back upon themselves. reflections smile too slowly. Whispers slide beneath birdsong like hidden knives. 

The realm itself is alive with glamour. Not merely illusion–but desire made manifest.

Ebonreach studies longing the way predators study weakness.

Flowers bloom according to memory. Music echoes from moments a traveller most aches to relive. Warmth settles against the skin like affection. The air smells of jasmine, old parchment, candle smoke, lost summers, first love.

Whatever someone wishes to find–Ebonreach becomes it.

And underneath every beauty waits corruption. 

Ebonspire– Capital of the Unseelie Spring Court, The Court of Shadows and Secrets.

At times the city appears carved from midnight stone veined with molten gold; at others, it transforms into radiant marble palaces crowned with stained -glass domes and sunlit towers. Entire streets alter between dusk and dawn. Walls dissolve into gardens. Corridors lengthen or narrow according to the will of Smerdante. 

Nothing is stable except power.

The city is layered with glamour so ancient and intricate that even its inhabitants sometimes forget which face is real.

By day Ebonspire gleams with impossible splendour: fountains singing with enchanted waters, balconies draped i sapphire silks, orchards heavy with luminous fruit, great halls glowing beneath floating constellations trapped under crystal ceilings.

The air is perfumed with roses, amber - resin and fresh rain. 

Music drifts everywhere. Soft violins. Whispering choirs. Laughter too beautiful to trust.

At night the glamour loosens. Gold darkens to bruised bronze. petals blacken at the edges. Perfume sours under the sweetness. Shadows peel back slowly from walls like living smoke.

And sometimes– for a heartbeat– the truth shines through. 

Cracks in the marble. Black roots within polished floors. Water running dark as ink. Eyes watching from mirrors.

The ruler of Ebonreach does not conquer through war alone. 

He seduces.

He tempts. 

He unravels until prisoners walk willingly into their own destruction. 

Deep in the bowels of the earth is The Witherdeep. A hidden prison not glamoured, not sparkling, not a flash of brilliance– A dank blackness waiting to pounce. 

In the darkness, vines twist and pull, earth captures and stems coil like serpents. 

Walls breathe damp decay. Shadows move where nothing lives. Ancient magic pools in the depths like poisoned water.  

Some say the Witherdeep is older than Ebonreach itself. 

Other whisper that Ebonspire was built about it deliberately– A beautiful mask laid carefully across an open wound. 

For that is the true nature of The Unseelie Spring Court:

not destruction openly declared, but corruption hidden beneath wonder. 

A hand offered gently.

A smile.

A promise. 

And the slow, exquisite moment when someone no longer remembers they were every trying to escape.
