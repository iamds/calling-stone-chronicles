The Unseelie Realm of Summer. The Mirelands, Realm of Rot and Decay. House Morvaine. it holds mire, decay, disease and destruction underneath the glamoured veil of a summer's day.  Mud rises into hands. Moss chokes marble and rot devours gold. it is a brutal place, possessive, primal. Beauty in any form is seen as something to claim. 

Like Smerdante, Thornhelm, king of Unseelie Spring, King of Mire, uses a garden as a snare. A beautiful rich, vibrant place, tended by a witch. Roots will ensnare, vines will hold, a tree will capture. And beneath it all lies the Witherdeep, an underground prison alive with roots, moss and decay.

Morvania/ The Mirelands — Realm of the Unseelie Summer Court
The realm of beauty that devours itself.

Morvania/ The Mirelands stretches beneath an endless late-summer sky stained gold, crimson, and bruised peach, where heat hangs unmoving and storms gather without ever fully breaking. The land appears breathtaking at first glance: vast orchards heavy with impossible fruit, rivers glittering like melted amber, gardens exploding with colour so vivid it almost hurts to look at.

But every perfection lingers a moment too long.

Flowers bloom until their petals split beneath their own weight. Fruit swells past ripeness and bursts sweet rot across marble paths. Vines creep not upward toward sunlight, but sideways, searching, listening, wrapping slowly around statues and walls as though trying to reclaim them.

The capital rises at the heart of the realm:

Morivantar— The BloomingMirage
A palace-city woven through the Giardino itself.

Morivantar is less built than grown. Ivory terraces curl from enormous living trees whose bark gleams gold in daylight and blackens subtly beneath moonrise. Delicate bridges of flowering vine span luminous pools where reflections move a heartbeat too slowly, smiling after the viewer has already turned away.

The city smells constantly of nectar, citrus blossom, honeywine, and decay hidden beneath perfume.

Everywhere, water gleams:
ornamental ponds smooth as mirrors,
fountains shaped like dancing maidens,
canals crowded with lilies large enough to cradle sleeping children.

And beneath every surface:
roots.

Watching roots.
Listening roots.

The Giardino surrounds the palace like a living dream. By day it appears impossibly beautiful — sculpted hedges, perfect roses, silver birdsong drifting through glowing leaves, sunlight fractured through glassfruit hanging from branches.

By night, the glamour loosens.

Petals darken at the edges.
Sap thickens black as oil.
Thorns lengthen silently.
The perfume deepens into something overripe and choking.

Nothing fully dies here.

It lingers.

Rotting beautifully.

The ruler of the Unseelie Summer Court , THornhelm, believe passion is truth stripped bare. He celebrates indulgence, temptation, longing, and possession. Music drifts endlessly through the courts — languid violins, slow drums, laughter that sounds warm until heard twice.

The realm is known for:

• Glamour gardens designed to entrance intruders and expose hidden desire.
• Bloommagic — enchantments tied to beauty, temptation, decay, and obsession.
• Living architecture that reshapes itself according to mood, hunger, and power.
• Mirrorwater pools capable of revealing longing instead of truth.
• Storm-feasts where nobles dance beneath violent lightning without fear.

Yet beneath every golden surface lies a terrible truth:

Unseelie Summer does not fear endings.

It fears emptiness.

So it consumes,
clings,
blooms,
smothers,
and burns—

until nothing remains except beauty collapsing beneath its own hunger.

And underneath it all the Witherdeep. A prison to frighten the most brave.
