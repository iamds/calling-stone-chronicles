Skyrenders are master hunters of the northern skies, using fog, cloud and storm fronts to conceal their approach. Rarely seen until the moment they strike, they descend from above with devastating precision, targeting weakness, hesitation and exposed flanks.

Unlike many predators, Skyrenders possess a frightening intelligence. They observe their prey from afar, studying patterns of movement and behaviour before committing to an attack. Travellers often report feeling watched long before they ever glimpse the creatures themselves.

Perhaps most feared are the living parasites carried within their claws. When a Skyrender strikes, these creatures can be embedded into a victim's flesh, burrowing beneath the skin and feeding upon blood and tissue. If not removed quickly, the infestation can spread rapidly and prove fatal.

Skyrenders rarely hunt alone. Instead, they work in coordinated pairs or small groups, circling above mountain passes and mist-filled valleys while communicating through eerie cries that echo across the frozen peaks.

*"Don't let them read you. They'll strike where you hesitate."* — Temerin

**Known Habitat**

• High mountain ranges
• Frozen cliffs and spires
• Storm fronts
• Mist-filled valleys
• Cloud-shrouded peaks

**Signs of Approach** • Fog moving against the wind
• Sudden silence among birds
• Shadows crossing the clouds
• Unexplained rushes of air

**Parasitic Creatures** 

Skyrenders carry living parasites embedded within their prey through puncture wounds. Once attached, these creatures burrow beneath the skin, feeding on blood and tissue while spreading deeper into the body. If not removed or expelled, they can prove fatal.
