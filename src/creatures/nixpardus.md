The Nixpardus is older than legend, myth and stories. Tavian tells Gracie the legend of The Nixpardus, when she is upset by Dara's attitude to her, turning the guards and most of the group against her, by suggesting she is a witch. 

Quinn stretches contentedly with a purr.

“Right. Story time. Try not to make it tragic for once, YourHighness.”

Tavian leans back, eyes narrowing.

Lifting his chin, his voice is soft but sure—like he’s repeating something etched into memory, not just words but almost a warning. *Not exactly bedtime cheer.*

“They say he was born before the snow first fell.

Not summoned. Not shaped. Not made.

Just…there.”

“The Nixpardus walks when the land forgets its noise.

When breath stills. When lies curl into frost.

He has no court. No pack. No need for titles.

He is not bound by crown or coin. No spell or sword will touch him.

 

He sees what others hide. Watches when they pretend.

Smells fear from behind polished words.”

Tavian’s eyes flick out of the tent. Dara stands alone, his dark hair white with snowflakes, cloak pulled tight. His eyes look towards the horizon. 

“And does not care for pride dressed as power.”

“They say his silence can strip the truth from you.

That one look can leave a man changed—or broken.

He does not hunt. He chooses.

 

And when he strikes…

There is no roar.

No warning.

Just a stillness so complete, it’s the last thing they feel.”

He lets the words hang there, quiet and heavy as the snowfall outside.

I swallow. The trees feel closer. The air, tighter. Like the forest has stopped breathing just to listen.

Beside me, Quinn stretches.

“Mm,” he purrs. “I like him. Doesn’t waste time chasing things that run. Just waits. Let’s them dig their own graves. And not bad, young prince, not bad.”

The fire outside, crackles low, casting long shadows.

I don’t speak.

The story echoes in my head. *That stillness. That silence.That sense of being seen.*

*Do I feel safer now? Or less.*

The snow stops.
