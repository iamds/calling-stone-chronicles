The Caudisguiba is also known as the Woodland Witch of Limskada. There are many legends and tales about her. She lives in Nightspill Hollow and is the first unnatural creature Gracie meets on the quest. She is, as Tavian says, something to be feared!

“Tell me, My Lady Grace… have you heard of the Caudisguiba?”

I frown. “The what?”

“The forest witch. Half woman, half beast. Wings like thunderclouds, eyes that flash like lightning, scales that catch moonlight and twist it into madness.”

“Sounds charming.”

“Oh, but she’s far more than charming,” Taviansays, straightening with mock seriousness. 

“They say she lives where the deep wood begins—Nightspill Hollow—where Limskada fades into shadow and no one dares follow. Her robe is the colour of a bruised sky, her tail coils like smoke. Her claws are ruby-bright and dipped in the venom of dreams.”

He places a hand to his chest, eyes wide. “And her hair! A tangle of serpents—or maybe just green fire. The tales change depending on who survived to tell them.”

Across the kitchen, one of the stableboys actually shudders.

Tavian grins.

“They say she snatches children who stray too far, whispers curses into the ears of travellers, and—if she likes you—lets you go mad slowly rather than all at once.”

“She sounds like a bedtime story designed to keep kids indoors,” I say flatly, though my insides have gone oddly cold.

“Oh, probably,” Tavian says breezily. “But stories start somewhere, don’t they?”

He winks and pops the last of my bread into his mouth, as if we haven’t just been talking about a clawed nightmare witch who might or might not live along the route we’re about to take.

“Besides,” he says, brushing crumbs from his tunic,“no quest worth anything starts without at least one monster in the woods.”

When they do encounter her, Gracie uses kindness to ensure they pass through the wood unscathed. Some monsters aren't monsters at all. Sometimes, they are an individual needing a friend.
