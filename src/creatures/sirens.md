The Sirens are creatures from Ancient Greek myth and legend. Said to lure sailors to their death by song, they are impossible to ignore. Many a sailor has lost his life due to their compelling voices. In Threads of Trial and Light, The Sirens are even more deadly. They recognise something in Gracie and want to possess her. She alone is able to withstand their tempting voices and only thanks to Quinn. 

From Book 1: 

“Sirens steal men’s minds,” Temerin says quietly. “Not with blades or brute force, but with song. A song so sweet it unravels thought, memory, will. Those who hear it lose themselves — some drown, chasing the voice. Others walk smiling into death.”

He glances towards the lake.

And when they arrive to charm the company on the lake, it is Gracie who fights back.
