Whitespine Hunters are relentless pack predators that haunt the frozen plains and mountain basins of the North. Drawn to movement, they instinctively target anything upright, visible, or attempting to flee. Those who survive encounters quickly learn that running only hastens death.

Their bodies move with disturbing, unnatural motions. Limbs twist and snap at impossible angles, while their forms flicker between solid flesh and wraithlike shadow. Their eyes hold no reflection, no shine, and no hint of life, giving the unsettling impression that they perceive the world through senses beyond sight.

Unlike ordinary predators, Whitespine Hunters operate according to a strict and deliberate pattern. One creature leads while the others wait, observing and circling before taking turns to strike.Understanding and disrupting this pattern is often the difference between survival and death.

They are most often encountered in regions of bitter cold far from civilisation, where silence reigns and the snow stretches unbroken across the landscape. Few travellers ever forget the sound of their shrieks echoing through the darkness.

**Habitat**

• Frozen plains
• High mountain basins
• Snow-choked valleys
• Areas of long, bitter cold
• Regions distant from settlements or light

**Signs of Approach** 

• Heavy, wet breathing
• Ear-splitting shrieks
• Dogs becoming silent and still
• Repeated howls in the darkness
• Unnatural stillness before movement

**Survival Notes** 

• Stay low. Crawl. Do not rise.
• Do not run. Movement feeds them.
• Break their pattern.
• Distractions can divide the swarm.
• Long periods of stillness weaken them.
• Magic can confuse or repel them.
