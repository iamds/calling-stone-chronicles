The embercage hangs low against Ciaran’s saddle, its dark metal dulled by ash and travel. Not quite a lantern, not quite a brazier — a small, wrought-iron cage of curved bars, each one etched with fine, twisting lines that catch the light when the coals stir.

Inside, the fire breathes— a slow, steady glow of deep orange and red, banked low beneath a cradle of blackened stone. As the wind breathes, the embers brighten. 

A hinged panel sits at the front, bound with a clasp worn smooth from use. When opened, heat spills out in a sudden rush, sharp and dry, carrying the scent of resin and old smoke.When closed, the cage dims again, holding the fire close, shielding it from the endless wind that scours the open land. Faint runes thread along the metal, keeping the coals alive when they should have died days ago.
