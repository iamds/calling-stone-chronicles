In Book Three, Ribbons of Destiny and Belief, Tavian experiences Gracie's anger. His response is contrition and sorrow. He gives her a beautiful ring, hoping she will accept, both him and his apology. 

"I see him in the glass — his cheek crimson, eyes hollowed with hurt. He steps closer, not reaching, not daring. 

“I made you feel like you were mine to possess. To imprison. That isn’t what I meant.”

The lump in my throat hardens. “But you said it. Just.Like. Thornhelm.”

His jaw tightens, shame flashing. “Gods, did you think that? You are not mine, Gracie. But I am yours. If… you’ll still have me.”

He drops onto one knee.

I blink. His hand opens. A ring —etched with starbursts, threads of light, frozen in silver.

“Not shackles. I promise. A keepsake.Yours to throw away if you ever choose.”

The window’s chill seeps deeper, but my chest swells with something warmer, brighter. 

I bite my lip, tears catching. “You really are hopeless, you know that?”

His mouth quirks, but his eyes are wet too.“Hopelessly yours— And you are quite terrifying when you’re angry.”

“Don’t forget it.”

I let him slide it onto my finger. 

WhenTavian and I return, every gaze fixes on us—at the golden thread sparking between us, and the frostlit ring on my hand.
