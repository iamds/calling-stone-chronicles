The envoy appears for the first time in Book 2. Sent by the Seelie Courts after a dangerous incident involving Gracie, he aims to 'assist' and 'assess'. 

From Book 2: 

The Envoy rides at the centre of three—two silent shadows flanking him in green-and-gold  silk livery. He is young, polished, the sort of handsome that looks practised rather than born. Dressed like a summer promise, in deep forest green shot through with gold, the weave so fine it catches light with every move. A sheen of silver trims the edges of his cloak, catching frost-glare like threaded moonlight. At his throat, a single sapphire clasp anchors the fabric, bright enough to echo a high summer sky. Even his gloves are made for show, pale cream leather that would never survive real work, the gold stitching curling like vines along the seams.

Perhaps the envoy is not all he claims to be.
