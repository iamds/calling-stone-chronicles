Crown Prince Dara is a brooding, stern quiet young man. Described as dark-haired, composed with a serious expression. He has deep green eyes.  He hates the Fae, who he believes took, kidnapped, his younger sister Aurelin when she was seven. He is heir to the throne of LImskada and is devoted to his realm, country, and royalty. He has little time for Tavian's stories and quick comments. They bicker and argue constantly. As Crown Prince, Dara leads the quest for The third Callisvaan. He is able to command the soldiers/ guards who travel with them. He is expecting Gracie to fail miserably on the quest. 

He is afraid of the Fae, witchcraft and magic. He only tolerates Quinn and Temerin, because they are useful to the quest and therefore the healing of his realm. 

His first reaction to Gracie, when he meets her in the Castle of Limskada is immediate and judgemental. 



Sitting at the head of the table, are the king and queen with two young men beside them.

 The king rises to his feet.

 “Welcome, My Lady. Welcome to our humble feast,”he says.

I glance around the long table, spread with platters of meat, poultry, fruits, and breads. *If this is humble, I canonly imagine what extravagant might look like.*

 “Thank you, Your Majesty,” I say. Temerinsmiles. *Seems I’ve said the right thing.*

  “These are my sons, My Lady. Prince Dara, myeldest, and Prince Tavian. Your quest is also theirs. They will accompany you.”

I take in the twostarkly different faces. Dara, dark-haired, composed, with a serious expression,while Tavian, with pale silver hair, brims with restless energy. Yet, bothshare the same intense green eyes, deep as a forest.

“Why do we have to take this… stranger on the quest?” Dara’s voice cuts through the silence.  “She’s an outsider. A girl. Weak. Fragile.”

            My temper flares. *Oh ,fantastic. Another person deciding I’m not good enough.*

  “My Lord Prince, I am neither fragile nor weak,” I say, my voice steady. “I havebeen chosen to help you on this quest.”

Dara sneers, his nostrils flaring with disdain. “Arrogant little—”

Temerin steps forward, his hand raised, to quell any further tension. “The Callisvaan has called her. Do not question its work, Prince Dara,” Temerin’s voice is firm, his eyes flickering between the brothers. 

 “And you, Gracie, this prince is sworn to protect his kingdom, just as his brother is. You will remember that.” 

*Right, because I’m the one with the problem, obviously.*

Dara stares at me, his gaze pinning me in place, arms folded, a silent wall between us.

 “Can you fight, girl? Can you wield a sword, a bow, or an axe?” he says.

I bow my head. “I have been trained MyLord.”

Dara raises an eyebrow. I catch Tavian’s eye across the table, and to my surprise, he smiles at me. I give a small one back.
